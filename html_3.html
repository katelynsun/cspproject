Rock - The Beatles

The Beatles were an English rock band consisting of four teenagers from Liverpool. The group performed at small venues in 1960 and 1961. When Brian Epstein saw the group play at the Cavern Club, he was curious after hearing mention of the band. He returned to take in a few more shows and on December 10, 1961, Epstein approached the band and a five-year contract was signed in January of 1962. The Beatles' first single, "Please Please Me," was recorded in November and released in January 1963. The song reached No. 1 in the UK.

Classical - Ludwig van Beethoven

Ludwig van Beethoven was a German painist and composer. His compositions were a combination of vocals and instruments. Beethoven life was marked by a struggle against deafness. As a child, Beethoven's father began teaching him music with an extraordinary rigor and brutality that affected Beethoven for the rest of his life. When the Holy Roman Emperor died in 1790, Beethoven received the honor of composing a musical memorial. He made his long-awaited public debut in Vienna on March 29, 1795. Beethoven's most famous piece is Für Elise.

Pop - Ariana Grande

Ariana Grande was born on June 26, 1993. She took to singing and acting at a young age. She got her first big break in 2008 when she was 15 years old. Two years later, she appeared in the musical "Cuba Libre". Grande got her breakout rold in Victorious, a Nickelodeon series. While busy with the show, she pursued a musical career. When Grande's character in "Victorious" went viral, she went to star in the spin-off "Sam and Cat". "The Way" was the first single from Grande's debut album, "Yours Truly". The 2014 release "My Everything" sold 169,000 copies in its first week, debuting at No. 1.
