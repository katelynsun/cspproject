Rock - The Beatles

The Beatles were an English rock band consisting of four teenagers from Liverpool. The group performed at small venues in 1960 and 1961. When Brian Epstein saw the group play at the Cavern Club, he was curious after hearing mention of the band. He returned to take in a few more shows and on December 10, 1961, Epstein approached the band and a five-year contract was signed in January of 1962. The Beatles' first single, "Please Please Me," was recorded in November and released in January 1963. The song reached No. 1 in the UK.

Classical - Ludwig van Beethoven

Ludwig van Beethoven was a German painist and composer. His compositions were a combination of vocals and instruments. Beethoven life was marked by a struggle against deafness. As a child, Beethoven's father began teaching him music with an extraordinary rigor and brutality that affected Beethoven for the rest of his life. When the Holy Roman Emperor died in 1790, Beethoven received the honor of composing a musical memorial. He made his long-awaited public debut in Vienna on March 29, 1795. Pop - Ariana Grande

Ariana Grande was born on June 26, 1993. She took to singing and acting at a young age. She got her first big break in 2008 when she was 15 years old. Two years later, she appeared in the musical "Cuba Libre". Grande got her breakout rold in Victorious, a Nickelodeon series. While busy with the show, she pursued a musical career. When Grande's character in "Victorious" went viral, she went to star in the spin-off "Sam and Cat". "The Way" was the first single from Grande's debut album, "Yours Truly". The 2014 release "My Everything" sold 169,000 copies in its first week, debuting at No. 1.

Country - Blake Shelton

Blake Shelton was born in Ada, Oklahoma, in 1976 and moved to Nashvillewhen he was 17 to become a country music songwriter. By 2001, he was a star with his No. 1 hit "Austin", holding its spot for five weeks. In 2007, Shelton made his first appearance on reality TV, appearing first as a judge on "Nashville Star" and then on "Clash of the Choirs". He went on to win several awards at the 2010 Grand Ole Opry. Success followed him for many years, eventually onto the judging panel of "The Voice" in 2011.

Jazz - Miles Davis

Miles Davis was born in 1926, in Alton, Illinois. His dad introduced him to a trumpet at age 13. Davis developed a talent to play the trumpet and emplasized playing it without vibrato. While in high school, he played professionally and was invited to play with Dizzy Gillespie and Charles Parker. In 1945, Davis dropped out of Juilliard and become a full-time jazz musician, recording his first song as band leader. In the '50s and '70s, Davis was drawn into drug abuse. He overcame the addiction and spent the '80s experimenting different styles. He died of pneumonia and respiratory failure in 1991. His recording with Jones brought him his final Grammy in 1993. The honor was just another testament to Davis' lasting influence on jazz.
