Explore the history and impact of each genre on the world today.

Rock

History:

  • During the 40s, the popular genres of blues and country music were combined using powerful electric guitar riffs and drums to create a more "aggressive" genre than the originals.
  • In the 50s, Chuck Berry, a pioneer of rock music, grew in popularity, which led to the growth of rock music as a whole.
  • People appreciated the aspect of defiance that rock music popularized, both to popular music of the past and to the conservative society of the time.

Impact:

  • Rock influenced many factors of life with the major changes affecting fashion, attitudes, and language. Even to this day you see people wearing shirts of famous rock bands like AC/DC, Queen, The Rolling Stones, etc.
  • Having appeared during a time of heavy segregation between whites and blacks in thw 1950's, it changed musical norms and opened a door to youth culture.
  • Many white rock 'n' rollers were able to build a ladder for black artists to enter into the musical world and it indicated one of the first steps of acceptance.
  • Rock promoted the idea of self-expression and individuality, encouraging people to embrace both their identities and freedom.

Pop

History:

  • With the early to mid 1900s came the rise of genres like rock, country, and jazz. With those came a newer genre that was a culmination of them all: pop music.
  • With the pop genre, the most ~popular~ songs in the mainstream could be categorized into a group with characteristics of all sorts of styles of music.
  • Due to the growing popularity of radio and television and music icons like Elvis Presley, music became much more mainstream and accessible.
Country

History:

  • Originated during the early 1900s, country music was a significant part of rural southern and western American cultures.
  • The genre's roots lie in folk music, ballads, and settlers in areas like the Appalachians.
  • Fiddlin' John Carson created one of the first popular country songs in the 1920s, which led to the further growth of the genre in mainstream music.
