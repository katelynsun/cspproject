Rock - The Beatles

  • English rock band of 4 teenagers from Liverpool
  • Performed at small venues in 1960 and 1961
  • When Brian Epstein saw the group play at the Cavern Club, he was curious after hearing mention of the band.He returned to take in a few more shows and on December 10, 1961, Epstein approached the band and a five-year contract was signed in January of 1962.
  • First single: "Please Please Me", recorded in November and released in January 1963; song reached No.1 in the UK

Classical - Ludwig van Beethoven

  • German pianist and composer; compositions were a combination of vocals and instruments
  • Beethoven's life was marked by a struggle against deafness
  • As a child, Beethoven's father began teaching him music with an extraordinary rigor and brutality that affected Beethoven for the rest of his life
  • When the Holy Roman Emperor died in 1790, Beethoven received the honor of composing a musical memorial
  • He made his long-awaited public debut in Vienna on March 29, 1795
  • Most famous piece: Für Elise

Pop - Ariana Grande

  • Born on June 26, 1993
  • She took to singing and acting at a young age
  • She got her first big break in 2008 when she was 15 years old.Two years later, she appeared in the musical "Cuba Libre". Grande got her breakout rold in Victorious, a Nickelodeon series. When Grande's character in "Victorious" went viral, she went to star in the spin-off "Sam and Cat".
  • First single: "The Way"; Debut Album: "Yours Truly"
  • The 2014 release "My Everything" sold 169,000 copies in its first week, debuting at No. 1.

Country - Blake Shelton

  • Born in Ada, Oklahoma, in 1976 and moved to Nashvillewhen he was 17 to become a country music songwriter
  • By 2001, he was a star with his No. 1 hit "Austin", holding its spot for five weeks.
  • In 2007, Shelton made his first appearance on reality TV, appearing first as a judge on "Nashville Star" and then on "Clash of the Choirs".
  • He went on to win several awards at the 2010 Grand Ole Opry.
  • Success followed him for many years, eventually onto the judging panel of "The Voice" in 2011.

Jazz - Miles Davis

  • Born in 1926, in Alton, Illinois
  • His dad introduced him to a trumpet at age 13
  • Davis developed a talent to play the trumpet and emplasized playing it without vibrato. While in high school, he played professionally and was invited to play with Dizzy Gillespie and Charles Parker.
  • In 1945, Davis dropped out of Juilliard and became a full-time jazz musician.
  • In the '50s and '70s, Davis was drawn into drug abuse. He overcame the addiction and spent the '80s experimenting different styles.
  • His recording with Jones brought him his final Grammy in 1993.
